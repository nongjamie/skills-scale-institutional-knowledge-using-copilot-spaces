# Stakeholder Communication Guide

## Purpose

Provide templates and best practices for consistent, clear communication with different stakeholder groups throughout the project lifecycle. This guide ensures transparency, builds trust, and keeps all stakeholders informed and aligned. Use these templates and guidance to communicate effectively with executives, project teams, customers, and support teams.

---

## Stakeholder Groups & Communication Needs

Understanding your stakeholder groups and their unique communication needs is essential for keeping everyone informed and engaged.

### Executive Sponsors

**Who They Are**: C-suite executives, VPs, or business unit leaders who provide strategic direction and final approval authority

**Their Focus**:

- Business impact and ROI
- Strategic alignment and competitive advantage
- Risks that could impact business outcomes
- Budget and resource utilization
- Timeline and delivery confidence

**Communication Frequency**: Monthly milestone reviews or on escalation (when issues arise)

**Communication Format**:

- Executive summaries (1-2 pages maximum)
- High-level dashboards with key metrics
- Decision documents with clear recommendations
- Milestone achievement updates

**Communication Style**:

- Focus on business outcomes, not technical details
- Lead with the headline (what they need to know first)
- Provide options and recommendations, not just problems
- Be concise and respect their time

---

### Project Stakeholders

**Who They Are**: Product leads, engineering managers, team leads, or representatives from dependent teams

**Their Focus**:

- Progress toward milestones and timeline
- Dependencies and cross-team impacts
- Key decisions and approval gates
- Resource constraints and blockers
- Risk management and escalation

**Communication Frequency**: Bi-weekly or at key gates (kickoff, planning, release)

**Communication Format**:

- Detailed status updates with metrics
- Decision documents and approval requests
- Risk registers and mitigation plans
- Dependency and integration documentation

**Communication Style**:

- Be specific with metrics and data
- Include context and rationale for decisions
- Highlight dependencies and escalations clearly
- Show impact on timeline and resources

---

### External Users / Customers

**Who They Are**: Product users, customers, customer success teams, or support personnel

**Their Focus**:

- Feature availability and what's new
- Value delivered and how to use new features
- Known issues and workarounds
- Support and documentation
- Roadmap and future capabilities

**Communication Frequency**: At release; ongoing feedback channels

**Communication Format**:

- Release notes and feature announcements
- In-app notifications and tooltips
- Blog posts or case studies
- Training materials and documentation
- Feedback surveys and satisfaction measures

**Communication Style**:

- Use plain language, avoid jargon
- Focus on user benefits, not technical implementation
- Celebrate wins and show progress
- Be transparent about known issues and limitations

---

### Internal Support / Operations Teams

**Who They Are**: Customer support, operations, DevOps, system administration, or training teams

**Their Focus**:

- Readiness to support new features
- Integration points and handoff procedures
- Known issues and workarounds
- Training and documentation needs
- Operational impact and dependencies

**Communication Frequency**: As needed during planning and pre-release (typically 2-4 weeks before launch)

**Communication Format**:

- Runbooks and troubleshooting guides
- Training materials and video walkthroughs
- Handoff checklists and procedures
- Known issues and FAQ documents
- Operational impact assessments

**Communication Style**:

- Be procedural and detailed
- Provide clear steps and decision trees
- Include examples and common scenarios
- Be proactive about risks and mitigation

## Communication Templates

Use these templates as starting points for your communications. Customize them based on your project, stakeholders, and communication style.

---

## Template 1: Weekly Status Update

**Purpose**: Keep project stakeholders informed of weekly progress, upcoming work, risks, and decisions needed

**Audience**: Project stakeholders, Product Lead, Sponsor (if escalations exist), Core team

**Frequency**: Weekly (same day/time each week for consistency)

**Format**: Email or Slack message

---

### Template

**Subject:** `[Project Name] — Weekly Status [Week of MM/DD]`
